About the Ministry of Transport

Our History

The Ministry of Transport of the Syrian Arab Republic was established in 1946, shortly after independence. Since its inception, the ministry has been responsible for developing and maintaining the country's transportation infrastructure, including roads, railways, ports, and airports.

Over the decades, the ministry has overseen significant developments in Syria's transportation network, connecting cities and rural areas, facilitating trade, and improving the quality of life for millions of citizens. Today, we continue to work towards modernizing our infrastructure and implementing sustainable transportation solutions.

Organizational Structure

Minister of Transport

The Minister oversees all ministry operations and sets strategic direction for transportation policy.

Deputy Ministers

Deputy ministers manage specific sectors including roads, railways, and maritime transport.

Directorate General

The Directorate General handles day-to-day administrative and operational functions.

Technical Departments

Specialized departments manage engineering, planning, and safety operations.

Our Commitment

"We are committed to building a modern, efficient, and sustainable transportation system that serves all Syrian citizens and supports economic development."

The Ministry of Transport is dedicated to continuous improvement, innovation, and excellence in serving the public. We work closely with international partners, adopt best practices, and invest in our workforce to ensure the highest standards of service delivery.
