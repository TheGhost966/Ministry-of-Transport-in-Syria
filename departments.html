Our Departments

The Ministry of Transport is structured into several key departments, each playing a vital role in managing and developing Syria's transportation sector.

Department of Road Transport

Responsible for the planning, development, and maintenance of the national road network, including highways, urban roads, and rural access roads. It also oversees road safety initiatives and traffic management.

  • Road network planning & construction
  • Maintenance & rehabilitation
  • Traffic management & safety

Department of Rail Transport

Manages the development, operation, and regulation of the railway system. This includes passenger and freight services, railway infrastructure, and ensuring adherence to national and international rail standards.

  • Railway infrastructure development
  • Passenger & freight services
  • Safety & regulation

Department of Maritime Transport

Oversees the country's ports, shipping lanes, and maritime activities. Its functions include port management, navigation safety, maritime security, and the regulation of shipping companies.

  • Port operations & management
  • Shipping regulation & safety
  • Maritime security

Department of Air Transport

Regulates civil aviation, manages airports, and ensures compliance with international air safety standards. It also facilitates air traffic control and the development of air transport policies.

  • Civil aviation regulation
  • Airport management
  • Air traffic control

Department of Planning & Studies

Conducts research, develops strategic plans, and provides statistical analysis for the entire transportation sector. It plays a crucial role in forecasting future needs and guiding policy decisions.

  • Strategic planning
  • Feasibility studies
  • Statistical analysis

Department of Legal Affairs

Provides legal counsel to the Ministry, drafts legislation, and ensures compliance with national and international laws related to transportation. It also handles legal disputes and regulatory enforcement.

  • Legal counsel & legislation
  • Regulatory enforcement
  • Dispute resolution
